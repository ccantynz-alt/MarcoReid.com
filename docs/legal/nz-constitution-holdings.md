# CONSTITUTION OF ALECRAE IP HOLDINGS LIMITED

**⚠ DRAFT — FOR ATTORNEY REVIEW BEFORE ADOPTION ⚠**

**New Zealand Companies Act 1993**

---

## 1. COMPANY NAME

The name of the company is **AlecRae IP Holdings Limited** (the "Company").

## 2. INTERPRETATION

2.1 In this Constitution, unless the context otherwise requires:

- **"Act"** means the Companies Act 1993 (NZ) and any amendment or
  re-enactment thereof;
- **"Board"** means the board of directors of the Company;
- **"Director"** means a director of the Company for the time being;
- **"IP"** means intellectual property, including but not limited to
  software, trademarks, patents, trade secrets, copyrights, domain names,
  and all associated rights;
- **"Licence Agreement"** means any intellectual property licence agreement
  between the Company and a Licensee;
- **"Licensee"** means any entity to which the Company has granted a
  licence to use its intellectual property;
- **"Shareholder"** means a shareholder of the Company for the time being;
- **"Shares"** means ordinary shares in the Company.

2.2 Terms defined in the Act have the same meaning in this Constitution
    unless otherwise defined.

2.3 The provisions of the Act apply to the Company except to the extent
    that they are negated or modified by this Constitution.

## 3. COMPANY PURPOSE AND RESTRICTIONS

3.1 The principal purpose of the Company is to:
    (a) hold, manage, protect, and licence intellectual property;
    (b) register and maintain trademarks, patents, and domain names;
    (c) receive royalty and licence fee income from Licensees;
    (d) enforce intellectual property rights against infringers.

3.2 **The Company shall NOT:**
    (a) enter into customer-facing contracts for the supply of services;
    (b) employ operational staff (other than directors and minimal
        administrative support);
    (c) incur operational debts beyond those necessary for IP management;
    (d) guarantee the debts or obligations of any Licensee or any other
        entity;
    (e) grant security over its intellectual property assets without a
        Special Resolution of shareholders.

3.3 The restrictions in clause 3.2 are fundamental to the Company's
    purpose of protecting intellectual property through liability isolation.
    Any breach of these restrictions by a Director is a breach of the
    Director's duties under the Act.

## 4. SHARES

4.1 The Company may issue ordinary shares on such terms as the Board
    determines, subject to the Act and this Constitution.

4.2 All shares rank equally in all respects, including:
    (a) the right to vote at meetings of shareholders;
    (b) the right to an equal share in dividends authorised by the Board;
    (c) the right to an equal share in the distribution of surplus assets
        on liquidation.

4.3 No shares may be issued, transferred, or disposed of to any person
    without the prior written approval of the Board by unanimous resolution.

4.4 The Company shall maintain a share register in accordance with the Act.

## 5. TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

5.1 No shareholder may transfer, sell, assign, pledge, charge, or otherwise
    dispose of any shares without:
    (a) first offering the shares to the existing shareholders in
        proportion to their existing holdings (pre-emptive rights); and
    (b) obtaining the prior written approval of the Board by unanimous
        resolution.

5.2 Pre-emptive rights procedure:
    (a) The transferring shareholder must give written notice to the
        Company specifying the number of shares, the proposed price,
        and the proposed transferee;
    (b) The Company must notify the other shareholders within 10 business
        days;
    (c) The other shareholders have 20 business days to exercise their
        pre-emptive rights;
    (d) If pre-emptive rights are not exercised in full, the transferring
        shareholder may transfer the remaining shares to the proposed
        transferee at the proposed price, subject to Board approval.

5.3 No transfer shall be registered unless the transferee has first
    agreed in writing to be bound by this Constitution.

## 6. DIRECTORS

6.1 The minimum number of Directors is one (1). The maximum number is
    five (5).

6.2 Directors are appointed and removed by ordinary resolution of
    shareholders.

6.3 A Director may be removed by special resolution of shareholders in
    accordance with section 156 of the Act.

6.4 Directors are not required to hold shares in the Company.

## 7. POWERS AND DUTIES OF DIRECTORS

7.1 The business and affairs of the Company shall be managed by or under
    the direction of the Board in accordance with the Act and this
    Constitution.

7.2 The Board shall ensure that the Company does not breach the
    restrictions set out in clause 3.2.

7.3 The Board shall ensure that:
    (a) all intellectual property is properly registered and maintained;
    (b) all Licence Agreements are at arm's length and comply with
        transfer pricing requirements;
    (c) corporate formalities are rigorously maintained (minutes,
        resolutions, separate accounting);
    (d) the Company maintains separate bank accounts from all Licensees
        and other entities;
    (e) no funds are commingled with any Licensee or other entity.

7.4 **Protective Provisions — Requiring Unanimous Board Resolution:**
    The following actions require the unanimous approval of all Directors:
    (a) granting any new Licence Agreement;
    (b) terminating any existing Licence Agreement;
    (c) assigning, transferring, or encumbering any intellectual property;
    (d) entering into any contract with a value exceeding $10,000 NZD;
    (e) borrowing money or granting security over any assets;
    (f) appointing or removing any officer or agent;
    (g) making any distribution to shareholders;
    (h) amending this Constitution.

## 8. BOARD MEETINGS

8.1 The Board shall meet at least twice per calendar year.

8.2 A Director may convene a meeting of the Board by giving at least
    5 business days' notice to all Directors.

8.3 The quorum for a Board meeting is a majority of Directors, or one (1)
    Director if there is only one Director.

8.4 Board meetings may be held by telephone, video conference, or any other
    means of communication that allows all participants to communicate
    with each other simultaneously.

8.5 A resolution in writing signed by all Directors is as valid as if it
    had been passed at a meeting of the Board.

8.6 Minutes of all Board meetings and written resolutions must be kept
    and maintained for the life of the Company.

## 9. SHAREHOLDER MEETINGS

9.1 Annual meetings of shareholders shall be held in accordance with the
    Act.

9.2 Special meetings may be called by the Board or by shareholders holding
    at least 5% of the shares.

9.3 At least 10 business days' notice shall be given for any meeting of
    shareholders.

9.4 The quorum for a meeting of shareholders is the holder or holders of
    a majority of the shares.

9.5 Each share carries one vote.

9.6 A special resolution requires approval by 75% of the votes of
    shareholders entitled to vote and voting.

## 10. DIVIDENDS

10.1 The Board may authorise dividends in accordance with sections 52-55
     of the Act.

10.2 Before authorising any dividend, the Board must be satisfied that
     the Company will satisfy the solvency test in section 4 of the Act
     immediately after the distribution.

10.3 No dividend shall be paid unless all Licence Fee invoices have been
     issued and the Company has sufficient reserves to meet its obligations
     for the next 12 months.

## 11. INDEMNITY AND INSURANCE

11.1 The Company shall indemnify each Director against liability to any
     person other than the Company or a related company, to the extent
     permitted by section 162 of the Act.

11.2 The Company shall, to the extent permitted by the Act, effect and
     maintain Directors and Officers (D&O) liability insurance.

## 12. LIQUIDATION

12.1 On liquidation, the assets of the Company shall be applied:
     (a) first, in payment of the costs of liquidation;
     (b) second, in payment of preferential claims in accordance with
         the Act;
     (c) third, in payment of all other debts and liabilities;
     (d) fourth, the surplus (if any) shall be distributed to shareholders
         in proportion to their shareholdings.

12.2 Intellectual property shall not be sold to a competitor of any
     Licensee without first offering it to the Licensees at fair market
     value.

## 13. AMENDMENT

13.1 This Constitution may be amended by special resolution of shareholders,
     provided that any amendment to clauses 3.2, 5, or 7.4 (protective
     provisions) requires the unanimous approval of all shareholders.

---

**⚠ NOTES FOR ATTORNEY:**

1. This constitution is designed for a pure IP holding company. The
   restrictions in clause 3.2 are critical for maintaining liability
   isolation. Review whether additional restrictions are needed.

2. Consider whether the Company should be structured with different
   classes of shares (e.g., voting and non-voting) if future investors
   or co-founders are contemplated.

3. The pre-emptive rights mechanism (clause 5) is designed to prevent
   dilution and maintain control. Review for compliance with the Act
   and any potential investor concerns.

4. The protective provisions in clause 7.4 are designed for a sole-
   director/sole-shareholder structure. If additional directors or
   shareholders are added, these may need adjustment.

5. Under the Companies Act 1993, a company is not required to have a
   constitution. However, for an IP holding company, a constitution
   with explicit restrictions is strongly recommended to document the
   company's limited purpose and protect against scope creep.

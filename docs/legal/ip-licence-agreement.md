## AGREEMENT

### 1. Definitions

1.1 **"Licensed IP"** means all intellectual property owned by the Licensor
    relating to the AlecRae platform, including but not limited to:
    (a) all software, source code, object code, APIs, and databases;
    (b) all trademarks, service marks, trade names, and associated goodwill;
    (c) all patents and patent applications;
    (d) all copyrightable works;
    (e) all trade secrets and know-how;
    (f) all documentation and specifications;
    as more fully described in Schedule A of the IP Assignment Agreement
    between Craig Cantyn and the Licensor.

1.2 **"Territory"** means:
    [SELECT ONE PER AGREEMENT]
    - (a) New Zealand, Australia, and the Pacific Islands (for AlecRae Ltd NZ)
    - (b) The United States of America and Canada (for AlecRae Inc US)
    - (c) Australia (for AlecRae Pty Ltd AU — when formed, supersedes NZ licence for AU)
    - (d) The United Kingdom and the European Union (for AlecRae Ltd UK)

1.3 **"Licence Fee"** means the royalty payable by the Licensee to the
    Licensor as set out in Section 4.

1.4 **"Net Revenue"** means the Licensee's gross revenue from all sources
    (subscriptions, transaction fees, premium tiers, API access, and any
    other revenue derived from the Licensed IP) less: (a) refunds actually
    paid; (b) sales taxes, VAT, and GST collected and remitted; and
    (c) chargebacks and payment processor fees.

1.5 **"Term"** means the initial term and any renewal terms as set out in
    Section 6.

### 2. Grant of Licence

2.1 The Licensor grants to the Licensee an **exclusive** licence to use the
    Licensed IP in the Territory for the purpose of:
    (a) operating, marketing, distributing, and supporting the AlecRae
        platform to customers in the Territory;
    (b) sublicensing access to the platform to end users via SaaS
        subscription agreements;
    (c) using the trademarks in marketing, advertising, and promotional
        materials within the Territory;
    (d) making modifications, improvements, and localisations to the
        software as necessary for the Territory, subject to Section 3.

2.2 The licence is exclusive within the Territory, meaning the Licensor
    shall not grant any other licence for the Territory during the Term,
    and the Licensor shall not itself operate in the Territory in
    competition with the Licensee.

2.3 The Licensee shall not sublicense, assign, or transfer the licence
    (or any part thereof) to any third party without the prior written
    consent of the Licensor, except for end-user SaaS subscriptions
    granted in the ordinary course of business.

### 3. Improvements and New IP

3.1 Any improvement, modification, enhancement, derivative work, or new
    intellectual property created by the Licensee using or based on the
    Licensed IP (**"Improvements"**) shall be owned by the Licensor and
    shall automatically form part of the Licensed IP.

3.2 The Licensee hereby assigns to the Licensor all right, title, and
    interest in and to any Improvements, and shall execute all documents
    necessary to perfect such assignment.

3.3 The Licensor shall make all Improvements available to all Licensees
    (i.e., all operating companies) for use in their respective Territories.

3.4 [NOTE TO ATTORNEY: This clause ensures all IP flows back to Holdings
    regardless of which operating company develops it. This is critical
    for maintaining the integrity of the IP holding structure. Consider
    whether the Licensee should receive any credit, reduced royalty, or
    other consideration for Improvements it develops.]

### 4. Licence Fee

4.1 In consideration of the licence granted herein, the Licensee shall
    pay to the Licensor a licence fee (the **"Licence Fee"**) calculated
    as follows:

    **[OPTION A — Percentage of Net Revenue]**
    [X]% of the Licensee's Net Revenue per calendar quarter.

    **[OPTION B — Fixed Fee Plus Percentage]**
    A fixed fee of $[AMOUNT] per calendar quarter, plus [X]% of the
    Licensee's Net Revenue exceeding $[THRESHOLD] per quarter.

    **[OPTION C — Fixed Annual Fee]**
    A fixed fee of $[AMOUNT] per calendar year, payable quarterly in
    advance.

4.2 [NOTE TO ATTORNEY: The royalty rate must be set at arm's length to
    comply with transfer pricing rules in all jurisdictions. A typical
    IP royalty rate for software is 5-20% of net revenue, but this must
    be supported by a transfer pricing study or benchmarking analysis.
    The rate should be consistent across all operating companies
    (adjusted for territory risk/size if needed). Engage a transfer
    pricing specialist to set this rate.]

4.3 Licence Fees shall be paid quarterly within 30 days of the end of
    each calendar quarter. All payments shall be made in New Zealand
    Dollars (NZD) unless otherwise agreed.

4.4 The Licensee shall provide the Licensor with a quarterly statement
    of Net Revenue, certified by a director of the Licensee, within
    30 days of the end of each calendar quarter.

4.5 The Licensor shall have the right, at its own expense, to audit the
    Licensee's books and records once per calendar year to verify the
    accuracy of Net Revenue statements. If an audit reveals an
    underpayment of more than 5%, the Licensee shall bear the cost
    of the audit.

4.6 Withholding tax: Where the Licensee is required by law to withhold
    tax on royalty payments, the Licensee shall withhold the required
    amount and pay the net amount to the Licensor, providing the
    Licensor with a withholding tax certificate. [NOTE: NZ-US, NZ-AU,
    and NZ-UK double tax agreements may reduce or eliminate withholding
    on royalties. The tax advisor must determine the applicable rates
    and ensure appropriate treaty relief is claimed.]

### 5. Quality Control and Brand Standards

5.1 The Licensee shall use the Licensed IP only in accordance with the
    brand guidelines and quality standards established by the Licensor
    from time to time.

5.2 The Licensee shall not modify, alter, or use the trademarks in any
    manner that dilutes, tarnishes, or brings into disrepute the
    Licensor's trademarks or brand.

5.3 The Licensee acknowledges that the Licensor's trademarks and brand
    reputation are of critical value, and any use of the trademarks
    shall inure to the benefit of the Licensor.

### 6. Term and Termination

6.1 This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for an
    initial term of [5/10] years (the **"Initial Term"**).

6.2 After the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for
    successive [1/2]-year periods unless either party gives at least
    [90/180] days' written notice of non-renewal before the end of the
    then-current term.

6.3 The Licensor may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice
    if the Licensee:
    (a) fails to pay any Licence Fee within 60 days of its due date;
    (b) commits a material breach of this Agreement that is not remedied
        within 30 days of written notice;
    (c) enters into liquidation, receivership, administration, or any
        analogous insolvency proceeding;
    (d) is acquired by, merges with, or comes under the control of a
        competitor of the Licensor or any other Licensee;
    (e) uses the Licensed IP in a manner that materially damages the
        reputation or goodwill of the Licensor's brand.

6.4 Upon termination:
    (a) the Licensee's right to use the Licensed IP ceases immediately;
    (b) the Licensee shall cease all marketing, distribution, and use of
        the trademarks within 30 days;
    (c) the Licensee shall provide reasonable assistance to transition
        existing customers to the Licensor or another Licensee;
    (d) all accrued but unpaid Licence Fees become immediately due;
    (e) the Licensee shall return or destroy all copies of the Licensed IP
        in its possession, and certify destruction in writing.

### 7. Intellectual Property Protection

7.1 The Licensee shall promptly notify the Licensor of any infringement,
    misappropriation, or unauthorised use of the Licensed IP that comes
    to its attention.

7.2 The Licensor shall have the primary right to take action against
    infringers. The Licensee shall cooperate with and assist the Licensor
    in any such action at the Licensor's expense.

7.3 The Licensee shall not challenge or contest the Licensor's ownership
    of the Licensed IP during or after the Term.

### 8. Confidentiality

8.1 Each party shall keep confidential all information of a confidential
    nature received from the other party in connection with this Agreement,

    prior written consent of the disclosing party.

8.2 The terms of this Agreement (including the Licence Fee) are confidential
    and shall not be disclosed to any third party except to the parties'
    professional advisors, auditors, and tax authorities.

### 9. Limitation of Liability

9.1 The Licensor's total liability under or in connection with this

    in the 12 months preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

9.2 Neither party shall be liable for any indirect, consequential, special,
    or punitive damages arising out of this Agreement.

10.2 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall

**⚠ NOTES FOR ATTORNEY REVIEW:**

1. **Transfer pricing** — The royalty rate (Section 4) is the most critical
   commercial and tax decision in this agreement. It must be at arm's
   length and defensible under the transfer pricing rules of NZ (Transfer
   Pricing Guidelines, s GC 6-14 Income Tax Act 2007), the US (IRC §482),
   Australia (Division 815), and the UK (TIOPA 2010 Part 4). A transfer
   pricing benchmarking study is strongly recommended.

2. **Withholding tax** — Royalties paid from US/AU/UK to NZ may be subject
   to withholding tax. NZ has double tax agreements with all target
   jurisdictions that may reduce rates:
   - NZ-US: 5% withholding on royalties (Article 12)
   - NZ-AU: 5% withholding on royalties (Article 12)
   - NZ-UK: 10% withholding on royalties (Article 12)
   The tax advisor must confirm these rates and ensure treaty relief is
   properly claimed.

3. **Craig signing for both parties** — As sole director of both entities,
   Craig will sign on behalf of both the Licensor and Licensee. This is
   legally permissible but should be documented in board minutes for both
   entities, with the conflict of interest noted and managed.

4. **Exclusive vs. sole licence** — This draft uses "exclusive", meaning
   even the Licensor cannot operate in the Territory. Consider whether
   "sole" (Licensor can also operate) is more appropriate, particularly
   if Holdings might licence to a different entity in the future.

5. **Improvements clause** — Section 3 assigns all Improvements back to
   Holdings. This is essential for the IP holding structure but may have
   implications if external developers or employees of the operating
   company create improvements. Ensure this is consistent with employment
   agreements and contractor IP assignment agreements.

6. **Insolvency provisions** — The termination on insolvency clause
   (Section 6.3(c)) may be affected by insolvency law in some
   jurisdictions (e.g., ipso facto clauses may be unenforceable under
   Australian insolvency law reforms). Attorney should review per
   jurisdiction.

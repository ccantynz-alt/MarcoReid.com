## AGREEMENT

### 1. Definitions

1.1 **"Assigned IP"** means all intellectual property described in Schedule 1,
    including but not limited to:

   (a) All software source code, object code, algorithms, machine learning
       models, training methodologies, databases, data structures, APIs,
       and related documentation;

   (b) All trademarks, service marks, trade names, brand names, logos,
       slogans, and associated goodwill, whether registered or unregistered,
       including the marks "AlecRae", "The Oracle", "AlecRae Legal",
       "AlecRae Accounting", "AlecRae Voice", and "AlecRae Courtroom";

   (c) All domain names, including alecrae.com, alecrae.accountant, and
       alecrae.legal (when acquired);

   (d) All inventions, discoveries, improvements, processes, methods,
       techniques, and know-how, whether or not patentable;

   (e) All copyrightable works, including but not limited to website content,
       marketing materials, documentation, user interface designs, and
       graphic designs;

   (f) All trade secrets, confidential information, proprietary processes,
       and business methods;

   (g) All rights to apply for or register any of the foregoing in any
       jurisdiction worldwide;

   (h) All rights to sue or recover damages for past, present, or future
       infringement, misappropriation, or violation of any of the foregoing.

1.2 **"Effective Date"** means the date of execution of this Agreement.

### 2. Assignment

2.1 The Assignor hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to the
    Assignee, with full title guarantee, all right, title, and interest in
    and to the Assigned IP, including all rights to sue and recover damages
    for past, present, and future infringement or misappropriation, free
    and clear of all liens, encumbrances, security interests, and claims
    of any kind.

2.2 The assignment is effective worldwide and includes all rights under the
    laws of New Zealand, the United States, Australia, the United Kingdom,
    the European Union, and all other jurisdictions.

2.3 The assignment includes all renewals, extensions, continuations,
    divisions, reissues, and foreign counterparts of any registered or
    pending intellectual property rights.

### 3. Consideration

3.1 In consideration of the assignment, the Assignee shall:

   (a) Issue [NUMBER] ordinary shares in the Assignee to the Assignor; and/or

   (b) Pay the sum of $[AMOUNT] NZD to the Assignor;

   (c) [NOTE TO ATTORNEY: The consideration structure should be determined
       by the tax advisor. Options include shares, cash, loan notes, or a
       combination. The consideration must be sufficient to support the
       assignment and should be structured to minimise tax consequences
       for both parties.]

3.2 The parties acknowledge that the consideration represents fair market
    value for the Assigned IP as at the Effective Date.

### 4. Further Assurances

4.1 The Assignor shall, at the Assignee's request and expense, execute all
    further documents, instruments, and assignments, and take all further
    actions, as may be reasonably necessary to perfect, confirm, or record
    the Assignee's ownership of the Assigned IP in any jurisdiction,
    including but not limited to:

   (a) Executing trademark assignment forms for filing with IPONZ, USPTO,
       and any other trademark office;

   (b) Executing patent assignment forms for filing with USPTO and any
       other patent office;

   (c) Executing domain name transfer authorisations;

   (d) Executing copyright assignment forms;

   (e) Providing declarations, affidavits, or statutory declarations as
       required by any intellectual property office.

### 5. Representations and Warranties

5.1 The Assignor represents and warrants that:

   (a) The Assignor is the sole and exclusive owner of the Assigned IP
       and has full right, power, and authority to assign it;

   (b) The Assigned IP is free and clear of all liens, encumbrances,
       security interests, licences, and claims of any kind;

   (c) No third party has any right, title, or interest in or to the
       Assigned IP, whether by contract, employment, engagement, or
       otherwise;

   (d) The Assignor has not previously assigned, transferred, pledged,
       or encumbered any of the Assigned IP;

   (e) To the Assignor's knowledge, the Assigned IP does not infringe
       the intellectual property rights of any third party;

   (f) There are no pending or threatened claims, actions, or proceedings
       relating to the Assigned IP;

   (g) All persons who contributed to the creation of the Assigned IP
       (if any) have assigned their rights to the Assignor or directly
       to the Assignee, and the Assignor will provide copies of such
       assignments upon request;

   (h) The Assignor has not used any open-source software in a manner
       that would impose open-source licensing obligations on the
       Assigned IP (e.g., copyleft/GPL obligations), or if such software
       has been used, full details are disclosed in Schedule 2.

### 6. Moral Rights

6.1 To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, the Assignor waives

6.2 Where moral rights cannot be waived under applicable law (including under

    omission that would otherwise infringe those moral rights.

**⚠ NOTES FOR ATTORNEY REVIEW:**

1. The consideration structure (Section 3) needs to be determined by
   the tax advisor. Key question: shares vs. cash vs. combination, and
   the tax implications of each under NZ law.

2. The non-competition clause (Section 7) should be reviewed for
   enforceability under NZ law. Given that Craig is the sole director
   and shareholder, the practical risk is low, but future scenarios
   (investors, co-founders, sale) should be considered.

3. The moral rights waiver (Section 6) should be reviewed under the
   Copyright Act 1994 (NZ). Moral rights cannot be assigned in NZ but
   can be waived in certain circumstances.

4. Consider whether a formal IP valuation is needed for tax purposes.
   If the IP is transferred at formation for nominal consideration
   (e.g., $1), this may have tax implications. If transferred for
   shares, the share value should be documented.

5. The open-source disclosure (Schedule 2) should be completed with a
   full audit of the codebase's dependency tree (npm audit, licence
   checker) before execution.

6. Consider whether this agreement should be a deed rather than a
   simple contract, for additional enforceability.
